# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Common questions about trading and this academy.

## 🎓 About Trading Academy

### Q: Is this course really free?
**A**: Yes! Trading Academy is completely free and open source. All content is available on GitHub under MIT license.

### Q: How long does it take to complete?
**A**: The full curriculum is designed for 18 weeks (4-5 months) at 10-15 hours per week. But you can go at your own pace.

### Q: Do I need programming experience?
**A**: Basic Python knowledge helps, but we teach what you need. If you're completely new to programming, spend 1-2 weeks learning Python basics first.

### Q: Can I skip modules?
**A**: We don't recommend it. Each module builds on previous ones. However, if you already know the basics, you can test out by completing the module exercises.

## 💰 About Trading

### Q: How much money do I need to start trading?
**A**: For paper trading (practice): $0
For live trading: Minimum $1,000-5,000 recommended. Never trade with money you can't afford to lose.

### Q: Can I really make money trading?
**A**: Yes, but it's difficult. Most beginners lose money. Success requires education, practice, discipline, and risk management. This academy teaches you how, but there are no guarantees.

### Q: How much can I make trading?
**A**: Realistic expectations:
- Beginners: -20% to +10% per year (learning phase)
- Intermediate: 10-30% per year
- Advanced: 30-100%+ per year
- Professional: Varies widely

**Warning**: Anyone promising guaranteed returns is lying.

### Q: Should I quit my job to trade?
**A**: NO! Not until you're consistently profitable for at least 2-3 years. Keep your day job and trade part-time initially.

### Q: Is day trading better than swing trading?
**A**: Neither is "better." Day trading requires more time and attention but offers quicker feedback. Swing trading is more suitable for people with jobs. Choose based on your lifestyle.

## 📊 Technical Questions

### Q: Which indicators are best?
**A**: There's no "best" indicator. Popular ones include:
- Moving averages (SMA, EMA)
- RSI
- MACD
- Bollinger Bands

The key is understanding how to use them, not collecting many indicators.

### Q: Do I need expensive software?
**A**: No. Free tools like TradingView (basic), Python, and Alpaca are sufficient to start. Upgrade only when you outgrow free tools.

### Q: What's the best timeframe to trade?
**A**: Depends on your strategy:
- Scalping: 1-5 minutes
- Day trading: 5-60 minutes
- Swing trading: Daily
- Position trading: Daily/Weekly

Higher timeframes are generally more reliable.

### Q: Should I use technical or fundamental analysis?
**A**: For short-term trading (days to weeks): Technical analysis
For long-term investing (months to years): Fundamental analysis
Many traders use both.

## 🤖 About Automation

### Q: Can I automate my trading?
**A**: Yes! That's what stock-agent-system is for. But automate only after you have a profitable manual strategy.

### Q: Will automated trading make me rich?
**A**: No guarantees. Automation removes emotions and allows 24/7 trading, but you still need a good strategy. Bad strategy + automation = faster losses.

### Q: Do I need to know Python?
**A**: For automated trading, yes. Python is the most popular language for trading. This academy teaches you what you need.

### Q: Can I use stock-agent-system for live trading?
**A**: Yes, but start with paper trading! The system supports both paper and live trading through various brokers.

## 💸 Risk and Money Management

### Q: How much should I risk per trade?
**A**: Rule of thumb: 1-2% of your account per trade. Never more than 5%.

Example: $10,000 account → Risk $100-200 per trade

### Q: Should I use leverage/margin?
**A**: Not recommended for beginners. Leverage amplifies both gains and losses. Master trading without leverage first.

### Q: What's a good win rate?
**A**: Win rate alone doesn't matter. A 40% win rate with 2:1 risk-reward is profitable.

Example:
- 40% win rate, 2:1 R:R
- 10 trades: 4 wins (+$800), 6 losses (-$600)
- Net: +$200 profit

### Q: How do I know when to exit a trade?
**A**: Plan before entering:
- Stop-loss: Exit if wrong (limit losses)
- Take-profit: Exit if right (lock in gains)
- Trailing stop: Let winners run

Never exit based on emotions.

## 🧠 Psychology

### Q: How do I control emotions while trading?
**A**:
1. Trade with money you can afford to lose
2. Use proper position sizing (1-2% risk)
3. Have a written trading plan
4. Keep a trading journal
5. Take breaks after losses
6. Practice with paper trading first

### Q: I keep losing money. Should I quit?
**A**: Most traders lose initially. Ask yourself:
- Am I following a plan?
- Am I keeping a journal?
- Am I learning from mistakes?
- Have I paper traded enough?

If yes to all, keep learning. If no, fix these first.

### Q: How long until I'm profitable?
**A**: Realistic timeline:
- 6-12 months: Understanding basics
- 1-2 years: Developing strategy
- 2-3 years: Consistent profitability

Some take longer, few take less. Be patient.

## 🎯 Strategy Questions

### Q: What's the best trading strategy?
**A**: There's no single "best" strategy. Good strategies:
- Fit your personality
- Match your time availability
- Have positive expectancy
- Are consistently applied

### Q: Should I follow trading signals/alerts?
**A**: No. Learn to trade yourself. Signal services:
- Don't teach you to trade
- Often have conflicts of interest
- Can't adapt to your risk tolerance

### Q: Can I copy successful traders?
**A**: Copying trades (social trading) can work short-term, but:
- You don't learn
- You don't know when to stop
- Their risk tolerance may differ from yours

Better to learn and develop your own edge.

## 🌍 Market-Specific

### Q: Can I trade US stocks from outside the US?
**A**: Yes, through international brokers like Interactive Brokers. Check your local regulations.

### Q: What about crypto trading?
**A**: Crypto trading is similar but:
- 24/7 markets (no sleep!)
- Higher volatility
- Different regulations
- Many scams

Start with stocks, then explore crypto if interested.

### Q: Should I trade forex?
**A**: Forex is different from stocks:
- 24/5 markets
- High leverage (dangerous)
- Currency pairs
- Different analysis

This academy focuses on stocks, but principles apply to forex.
