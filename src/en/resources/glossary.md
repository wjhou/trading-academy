# Trading Glossary

A comprehensive glossary of trading terms used throughout this book.

## A

**Alpha**: The excess return of an investment relative to the return of a benchmark index. Positive alpha indicates outperformance.

**Ask Price**: The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a security. Also called the "offer price."

**Asset Allocation**: The process of dividing investments among different asset classes (stocks, bonds, commodities, etc.).

**ATR (Average True Range)**: A volatility indicator that measures the average range between high and low prices over a period.

## B

**Backtest**: Testing a trading strategy on historical data to evaluate its performance before risking real capital.

**Bear Market**: A market condition where prices are falling or expected to fall, typically defined as a 20% decline from recent highs.

**Beta**: A measure of a security's volatility relative to the overall market. Beta of 1 means it moves with the market.

**Bid Price**: The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a security.

**Bid-Ask Spread**: The difference between the bid and ask prices. Narrower spreads indicate higher liquidity.

**Bollinger Bands**: A volatility indicator consisting of a moving average with upper and lower bands based on standard deviations.

**Breakout**: When price moves above resistance or below support, potentially signaling a new trend.

**Bull Market**: A market condition where prices are rising or expected to rise.

## C

**Candlestick**: A charting method that displays open, high, low, and close prices for a period in a visual format.

**Capital**: The funds available for trading or investing.

**Correlation**: A statistical measure of how two securities move in relation to each other. Ranges from -1 to +1.

**Covered Call**: An options strategy where you own the underlying stock and sell call options against it.

## D

**Day Trading**: Buying and selling securities within the same trading day.

**Delta**: An options Greek measuring how much an option's price changes relative to the underlying asset's price.

**Diversification**: Spreading investments across different assets to reduce risk.

**Drawdown**: The peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value, expressed as a percentage.

## E

**EMA (Exponential Moving Average)**: A moving average that gives more weight to recent prices.

**Entry Point**: The price level at which a trader opens a position.

**Exit Point**: The price level at which a trader closes a position.

## F

**Fill**: The execution of an order to buy or sell a security.

**Fundamental Analysis**: Evaluating securities by analyzing financial statements, management, competitive advantages, and economic factors.

## G

**Gamma**: An options Greek measuring the rate of change of delta.

**Gap**: A price level where no trading occurred, appearing as a space on a chart.

## H

**Hedge**: A position taken to offset potential losses in another investment.

**High-Frequency Trading (HFT)**: Automated trading strategies that execute a large number of orders at very high speeds.

## I

**Indicator**: A mathematical calculation based on price and/or volume used to forecast price movements.

**Iron Condor**: An options strategy involving four options at different strike prices, profiting from low volatility.

## K

**Kelly Criterion**: A formula for optimal position sizing based on win probability and risk-reward ratio.

## L

**Leverage**: Using borrowed capital to increase potential returns (and risks).

**Limit Order**: An order to buy or sell at a specific price or better.

**Liquidity**: The ease with which an asset can be bought or sold without affecting its price.

**Long Position**: Buying a security with the expectation that its price will rise.

## M

**MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)**: A trend-following momentum indicator.

**Margin**: Borrowed money used to purchase securities.

**Market Maker**: A firm that provides liquidity by continuously quoting buy and sell prices.

**Market Order**: An order to buy or sell immediately at the best available price.

**Mean Reversion**: A strategy based on the assumption that prices will return to their average over time.

**Momentum**: The rate of acceleration of price changes.

## O

**OBV (On-Balance Volume)**: A volume indicator that relates volume to price changes.

**Options**: Contracts giving the right (but not obligation) to buy or sell an asset at a specified price.

**Order Book**: A list of buy and sell orders for a security at different price levels.

**Overbought**: A condition where prices have risen too far too fast and may be due for a pullback.

**Oversold**: A condition where prices have fallen too far too fast and may be due for a bounce.

## P

**Paper Trading**: Simulated trading without risking real money, used for practice and testing.

**Position Sizing**: Determining how much capital to allocate to each trade.

**Put Option**: A contract giving the right to sell an asset at a specified price.

## R

**Resistance**: A price level where selling pressure is expected to overcome buying pressure.

**Risk-Reward Ratio**: The potential profit of a trade relative to its potential loss.

**RSI (Relative Strength Index)**: A momentum oscillator measuring the speed and magnitude of price changes.

## S

**Scalping**: A strategy involving many small profits from minor price changes.

**Sharpe Ratio**: A measure of risk-adjusted return, calculated as excess return divided by standard deviation.

**Short Position**: Selling a borrowed security with the expectation of buying it back at a lower price.

**Slippage**: The difference between expected and actual execution prices.

**SMA (Simple Moving Average)**: The average price over a specified number of periods.

**Spread**: The difference between bid and ask prices, or between two related securities.

**Stop-Loss**: An order to sell a security when it reaches a certain price, limiting losses.

**Straddle**: An options strategy involving buying both a call and put at the same strike price.

**Support**: A price level where buying pressure is expected to overcome selling pressure.

**Swing Trading**: Holding positions for several days to weeks to profit from price swings.

## T

**Technical Analysis**: Analyzing securities by studying price charts and patterns.

**Theta**: An options Greek measuring time decay.

**Tick**: The minimum price movement of a security.

**Trend**: The general direction of price movement (up, down, or sideways).

**Trend Following**: A strategy that attempts to profit from sustained price movements.

## V

**VaR (Value at Risk)**: A statistical measure of potential loss over a specific time period.

**Vega**: An options Greek measuring sensitivity to volatility changes.

**Volatility**: The degree of price variation over time. Higher volatility means larger price swings.

**Volume**: The number of shares or contracts traded during a period.

## W

**Walk-Forward Analysis**: A backtesting method that repeatedly optimizes on one period and tests on the next.

**Whipsaw**: Rapid price movements in opposite directions, often causing losses for trend followers.

## Y

**Yield**: The income return on an investment, expressed as a percentage.
