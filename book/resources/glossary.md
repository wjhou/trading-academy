# Trading Glossary 📖

A comprehensive dictionary of trading terms from beginner to advanced.

## A

**Ask (Offer)**: The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a stock.

**Asset**: Anything of value that can be owned or traded (stocks, bonds, real estate, etc.).

**ATR (Average True Range)**: A volatility indicator that measures how much a stock typically moves.

**Automated Trading**: Using computer programs to execute trades based on predefined rules.

## B

**Backtest**: Testing a trading strategy on historical data to see how it would have performed.

**Bar**: A representation of price movement over a time period, showing open, high, low, and close.

**Bear Market**: A market condition where prices are falling or expected to fall (typically 20%+ decline).

**Bid**: The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a stock.

**Bid-Ask Spread**: The difference between the bid and ask price.

**Blue Chip**: Large, well-established, financially sound companies (e.g., Apple, Microsoft).

**Bollinger Bands**: A volatility indicator with upper and lower bands around a moving average.

**Breakout**: When price moves above resistance or below support levels.

**Broker**: A licensed intermediary that executes trades on your behalf.

**Bull Market**: A market condition where prices are rising or expected to rise.

## C

**Candlestick**: A chart type showing open, high, low, and close prices in a visual format.

**Capital**: The money you have available to trade or invest.

**Circuit Breaker**: An automatic trading halt when markets drop too quickly.

**Close**: The final price at which a stock trades during a trading session.

**Commission**: Fee charged by a broker for executing a trade.

**Consolidation**: A period where price moves sideways, neither trending up nor down.

**Correction**: A decline of 10-20% from recent highs, typically temporary.

## D

**Day Order**: An order that expires at the end of the trading day if not filled.

**Day Trading**: Buying and selling stocks within the same trading day.

**Dividend**: A portion of company profits paid to shareholders.

**Drawdown**: The decline from a peak to a trough in your account value.

**DMA (Displaced Moving Average)**: A moving average shifted forward or backward in time.

## E

**EMA (Exponential Moving Average)**: A moving average that gives more weight to recent prices.

**Entry Point**: The price at which you buy a stock.

**Equity**: Ownership in a company (stocks) or the value of your trading account.

**Exchange**: A marketplace where stocks are bought and sold (NYSE, NASDAQ, etc.).

**Exit Point**: The price at which you sell a stock.

## F

**Fill**: When an order is executed and you receive the shares (buy) or money (sell).

**Fundamental Analysis**: Evaluating stocks based on financial data, earnings, growth, etc.

**Futures**: Contracts to buy/sell an asset at a future date at a predetermined price.

## G

**Gap**: When a stock opens significantly higher or lower than its previous close.

**GTC (Good-Till-Canceled)**: An order that remains active until filled or manually canceled.

**Golden Cross**: When a short-term moving average crosses above a long-term moving average (bullish).

## H

**Hedge**: A strategy to reduce risk by taking an offsetting position.

**High**: The highest price reached during a trading period.

**High-Frequency Trading (HFT)**: Using algorithms to execute many trades in fractions of a second.

## I

**Indicator**: A mathematical calculation based on price/volume used to predict future movements.

**IPO (Initial Public Offering)**: When a company first sells stock to the public.

## K

**Kelly Criterion**: A formula for optimal position sizing based on win rate and risk-reward.

## L

**Leverage**: Using borrowed money to increase position size (amplifies gains and losses).

**Limit Order**: An order to buy/sell at a specific price or better.

**Liquidity**: How easily an asset can be bought or sold without affecting its price.

**Long**: Buying a stock with the expectation that it will rise in value.

**Low**: The lowest price reached during a trading period.

## M

**MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence)**: A trend-following momentum indicator.

**Margin**: Borrowed money from your broker to trade larger positions.

**Margin Call**: When your broker demands more money because your account value dropped.

**Market Cap**: Total value of a company (share price × number of shares).

**Market Order**: An order to buy/sell immediately at the best available price.

**Market Maker**: A firm that provides liquidity by always offering to buy and sell.

**Mean Reversion**: The theory that prices tend to return to their average over time.

**Momentum**: The rate of price change; stocks moving strongly in one direction.

## O

**OHLC**: Open, High, Low, Close - the four key prices for a trading period.

**Open**: The first price at which a stock trades when the market opens.

**Options**: Contracts giving the right (not obligation) to buy/sell at a specific price.

**Order Book**: A list of all buy and sell orders for a stock.

**Overbought**: When a stock has risen too far too fast and may be due for a pullback.

**Oversold**: When a stock has fallen too far too fast and may be due for a bounce.

## P

**Paper Trading**: Simulated trading with fake money to practice without risk.

**Pattern**: Recognizable formations in price charts (head and shoulders, triangles, etc.).

**Pip**: The smallest price movement in forex trading.

**Position**: The amount of a stock you own (long position) or owe (short position).

**Position Sizing**: Determining how many shares to buy based on risk management.

**Price Action**: The movement of price over time, analyzed without indicators.

**Profit Target**: The price at which you plan to sell to take profits.

## R

**Rally**: A sustained increase in stock prices.

**Range**: The difference between high and low prices in a period.

**Resistance**: A price level where selling pressure prevents further price increases.

**Retracement**: A temporary reversal in the direction of a trend.

**Risk-Reward Ratio**: The potential profit compared to potential loss on a trade.

**RSI (Relative Strength Index)**: A momentum indicator measuring overbought/oversold conditions.

## S

**Scalping**: Very short-term trading, holding positions for seconds to minutes.

**Sell-Off**: A rapid decline in stock prices due to heavy selling.

**Sharpe Ratio**: A measure of risk-adjusted returns (higher is better).

**Short**: Selling a borrowed stock, expecting to buy it back cheaper.

**Slippage**: The difference between expected and actual execution price.

**SMA (Simple Moving Average)**: The average price over a specific number of periods.

**Sortino Ratio**: Like Sharpe ratio but only considers downside volatility.

**Spread**: The difference between bid and ask prices.

**Stop-Loss**: An order to sell if price drops to a certain level, limiting losses.

**Strategy**: A set of rules for when to buy and sell.

**Support**: A price level where buying pressure prevents further price decreases.

**Swing Trading**: Holding positions for days to weeks to capture price swings.

## T

**Take-Profit**: An order to sell when price reaches your profit target.

**Technical Analysis**: Analyzing price charts and patterns to predict future movements.

**Ticker Symbol**: A unique identifier for a stock (e.g., AAPL for Apple).

**Time Frame**: The period each bar represents (1-minute, daily, weekly, etc.).

**Trailing Stop**: A stop-loss that moves with the price to lock in profits.

**Trend**: The general direction of price movement (uptrend, downtrend, sideways).

**Trend Line**: A line connecting price highs or lows to visualize the trend.

## V

**Volatility**: How much and how quickly prices change.

**Volume**: The number of shares traded in a period.

**Volume Profile**: Analysis of volume at different price levels.

**VWAP (Volume-Weighted Average Price)**: Average price weighted by volume.

## W

**Watchlist**: A list of stocks you're monitoring for potential trades.

**Whipsaw**: When price quickly reverses direction, triggering stop-losses.

**Win Rate**: The percentage of trades that are profitable.

## Y

**Yield**: The income return on an investment (dividends/interest).
